Research Experience

Foundations in lab work, clinical genetics, and AI/regulatory literacy.

Undergraduate Laboratory Training

Through my undergraduate studies in Life Sciences at the University of Toronto, I developed a strong foundation in core experimental techniques across molecular biology, genetics, and chemistry. My coursework provided hands-on experience with:

  • Molecular Biology & Genetics: DNA extraction and purification, PCR amplification using targeted primers, agarose gel electrophoresis, Sanger sequencing, microscopy (brightfield & fluorescence), and basic cell culture techniques.
  • Biochemistry: Protein purification, enzyme kinetics, thin-layer and column chromatography, and spectrophotometry.
  • Data & Scientific Communication: Experimental design, statistical analysis, data interpretation, and formal scientific writing and presentation.

Bioinformatics & Genetic Research

I worked under Dr. André Mégarbané, Lebanon’s most renowned clinical geneticist and Chair of the Lebanese American University School of Medicine, on exome sequencing and bioinformatics analysis of rare hereditary disorders. My research focused on Woodhouse–Sakati Syndrome (WSS), a rare autosomal recessive disease caused by pathogenic variants in DCAF17, a gene involved in nucleolar function and E3 ubiquitin ligase activity.

Key contributions:

  • Analyzed 10+ patient exome datasets to identify and annotate pathogenic variants.
  • Assisted in PCR, gel electrophoresis, and Sanger sequencing to validate mutations.
  • Conducted variant annotation, data filtering, and genotype–phenotype correlation using tools such as VarSome and OMIM.

Publication: Wakim et al. “Woodhouse–Sakati syndrome: genotype–phenotype review and case of intra-familial heterogeneity.” Journal of Rare Diseases (2024).

Regulatory Affairs & AI in Healthcare

During my internship at Scopium AI, I conducted an experiential learning project examining global medical device regulations and the integration of AI in healthcare for a startup aimed at facilitating prediction and diagnosis of chest pain using AI.

Key outcomes:

  • Authored a comprehensive article on FDA regulatory pathways for AI-driven diagnostic devices, including Fast Track, Breakthrough Devices, and De Novo pathways.
  • Investigated strategies for navigating international regulatory frameworks (FDA, GDPR, MDR, NMPA).
  • Identified expedited approval mechanisms to support startup innovation.
  • Explored data privacy regulations, federated learning, and adaptive algorithm oversight in the context of global compliance.
