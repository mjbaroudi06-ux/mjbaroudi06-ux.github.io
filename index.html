About

My story is one of resilience, curiosity, and deliberate growth. From grasping the precision of oil painting to assisting in more than 20 complex surgeries, I have always sought environments that demand both technical skill and unwavering focus.

During my undergraduate studies, I forged a foundation in Health and Disease, Physiology, and Philosophy, fields that honed my ability to think critically through sustained engagement with dense research literature and intricate philosophical texts. This training sharpened my analytical reasoning and communication, allowing me to bridge disciplines and collaborate fluidly with clinicians, researchers, and engineers.

Along the way, I co-founded a startup advancing human-centric diagnostic innovation and launched UofT St. George’s first biotechnology club, now a community of hundreds driving real-world problem solving and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Whether it’s cliff jumps, skydives, or founding initiatives that didn’t exist before, I believe that growth lives in the calculated leap, and I’m always ready to take it.
